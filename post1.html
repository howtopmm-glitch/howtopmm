The Product Brief Every PMM Should Write

Published: September 2025 — by the Author

Every successful launch starts with alignment. The product brief is the PMM’s superpower: a one-page document that makes sure everyone — from product to sales to leadership — knows what we’re building, who it’s for, and why it matters.

What goes in a great brief?

  • Audience: Who the product is for, with one crisp persona statement.
  • Problem: The pain we’re solving (in one or two sentences).
  • Solution: What the product does and how it solves that pain.
  • Positioning: How it’s different from alternatives.
  • Success Metrics: How we’ll know it worked (adoption, pipeline, awareness).

Example (from our fictional SaaS, eSscents)

Audience: Growth marketers at mid-sized SaaS companies.
Problem: Messaging is inconsistent across campaigns.
Solution: eSscents’ “ScentSync” aligns brand language across every touchpoint.
Positioning: Unlike manual style guides, ScentSync updates automatically as campaigns evolve.
Success Metric: 80% reuse of approved messaging snippets in launch campaigns.

Keep it simple, keep it one page, and keep it shared. Your future self will thank you.
